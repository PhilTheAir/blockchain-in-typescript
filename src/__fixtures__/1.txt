They’ll be looking for you to clearly demonstrate your capability as a Lead/Senior Software Engineer by presenting your experience and skill set in a clear and structured way. The discussion will focus on assessing your fit for the role from both a technical and cultural perspective.
The team are sharp, highly engaged professionals who value passion, positivity, and strong communication skills.

For this role, exceptional communication is essential, so they’ll be paying close attention to how you articulate your experience and convey your ideas clearly and confidently.

From a communication perspective, many of the questions will be scenario-based, focusing on how you would approach specific situations. Wherever possible, try to draw on real-life examples from your experience; this will help paint a much more precise and more compelling picture for the interviewers.

From a technical standpoint, there will be focused questions based on both the job description and your resume, so it’s worth reviewing each carefully as part of your preparation, they will also draw in on your previous projects.

They’re particularly interested in someone who can dive deep into the technical details and provide specific examples of complex projects they’ve worked on, and also what you did as an individual. 

Other general questions they may ask you could be around how you approach problems, what is the most difficult technical problem you have faced and how did you solve it and what are your career aspirations.



Also - have you worked with stakeholders and vendors, and how you have managed difficult ones, or what do you do when you disagee with them, how would you approach this etc.



Other cultural questions to be aware of that are generally asked in these types of interviews are:
- strengths, weaknesses

- what you know about the company, what you like about them, 

- what you like about the role,

- why you want to leave your current role (never say money or talk bad about your company/manager), 

- what value you think you could bring to their team, 

- how you deal with conflict in a team, 

- examples of how you have overcome problems or conflict in the past, 

- what do you do if you disagree with your manager's decision etc.
